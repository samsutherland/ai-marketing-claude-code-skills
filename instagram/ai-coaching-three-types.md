## Script

Most cyclists think they just got an AI coach.

What they actually got is an AI that can describe the past.

This week Strava plugged into Claude.

You can now ask your training data anything. For free.

"How was my power last month." "Was that ride aerobic." It answers instantly.

That's genuinely useful. But it's not coaching.

I've spent 18 months building an AI cycling coach, and there are really three different things people call "AI coaching."

One is a formula. It takes your numbers and spits out a fitness score. It doesn't know you slept four hours.

Two predicts. It learns from your data and guesses your form. Closer. But it still hands you the same plan.

Three reads the mess. "I travelled, I'm cooked, I missed Tuesday" — and it actually rewrites the week. And tells you why.

Only the third one changes what you do tomorrow.

The first two describe you.

The Strava one is the first kind. It's a map of what already happened.

A map shows you the territory. It doesn't drive the car.

So before you trust any AI with your training, ask it one thing:

"My week fell apart. Now what?"

If it summarises, it's a dashboard.

If it changes the plan, that's a coach.

That gap is the exact thing Streeka is being built to close.
