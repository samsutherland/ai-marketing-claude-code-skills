## Version A — Observation-led

The AI isn't giving cyclists bad training advice.

It's giving great advice to someone else.

"What should I do to improve my FTP?" — the AI answers for a fictional cyclist with your rough goals. Not your history. Not your last three weeks. Not the four sessions you missed.

The fix is one thing: give it your actual numbers before you ask anything.

Compare:

Not: "Should I train today?"  
But: "Did 4.5h at threshold yesterday, slept 5h, HRV down 11%. Key session tomorrow. Easy ride, full rest, or shorten tomorrow?"

Not: "How do I prepare for a sportive?"  
But: "11 weeks. Longest ride is 4h. Event is 6h with 3000m climbing. I average 8h/week, sometimes 5. Where are the gaps?"

Not: "I missed a session, what now?"  
But: "Missed Tuesday's intervals. Wednesday and Friday are left, Thursday is rest. Intervals were the main stress session. Is there a version of Wednesday that rescues the intent without blowing up Friday?"

Same AI. The second question gets a real answer because it's actually about you.

## Version B — Personal story

I tried using Claude for my training earlier this year.

The first few weeks, the answers were useless. Not wrong — just generic. Built for someone with my rough goals, not my actual fitness, history, or schedule.

I nearly wrote it off.

Then I changed one thing: I started giving it my numbers before asking anything.

Same question. Completely different answer.

Before: "Should I train today?"  
After: "Did 4.5h at threshold yesterday. Slept 5h. HRV down 11%. Key session tomorrow. What's the call — easy ride, full rest, or push tomorrow out?"

Before: "Why am I not getting faster?"  
After: "8 months of training. FTP moved from 245 to 263w then stalled for 12 weeks. Mostly zone 2. About 80% of sessions completed. What am I missing?"

Before: "How do I prepare for my next event?"  
After: "11 weeks. Longest recent ride is 4h. Event is 6h with 3000m climbing. 8 good hours a week, 5 on a bad one. Where are the gaps?"

The AI handled all of these well. The constraint was never the AI — it was the context I was giving it.

I've written up the full cheat sheet: the prompts that work, the ones that don't, and what to change. Link in the comments.
