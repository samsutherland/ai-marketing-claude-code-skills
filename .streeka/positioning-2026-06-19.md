# Streeka Positioning — Dialled In (2026-06-19)

**Supersedes** the "Positioning (Finalised)" block in `session-notes.md` (2026-05-20).
**Why it changed:** Pillar 1 (disruption-as-default) is commoditising — Cycling Coach AI
now runs "rebuilds your week when life gets in the way" verbatim and out-ranks us
(see `ai-discoverability-reaudit-2026-06-17.md`). The defensible wedge moved to
*conversation* + *explainability*. Decisions taken with Sam 2026-06-19:
lead hero with conversation/explains; keep the anti-metrics contrast soft & implied.

---

## The three pillars (ranked)

1. **Conversation** — you chat with it; it's not a dashboard you operate.
2. **Explains every decision** — it tells you *why* the plan changed (anti black-box).
3. **Adapts to the messy week** — life/work/kids get in the way; disruption is the default.

Pillar 3 was the old lead. It's now third because it's the copyable one. Pillars 1 & 2
are harder to fake and answer the two live objections: TrainingPeaks' wall of numbers,
and the "AI black box" fear (validated in r/Velo).

---

## Positioning statement

> For desk-job cyclists who train seriously but can't predict their week, Streeka is an
> AI coach you chat with — it rebuilds your plan when life gets in the way, and explains
> every change so you always know why. Unlike TrainingPeaks (a tool you're left to
> interpret yourself) and JOIN (a rigid generator that assumes a clean week), Streeka
> works it through with you in plain language instead of leaving you to decode a screen
> full of numbers.

## Hero

**Lead one-liner:**
> An AI cycling coach you can actually chat with — that tells you why.

**Supporting sub-line (carries the proven messy-week hook):**
> It rebuilds your plan when work, family, or a bad week get in the way — and explains
> every call, so you're never guessing and never trusting a black box.

**Keep in rotation** (proven reach hook — the 127k reel line; now secondary / social):
> Training that survives the week you actually get.

## Differentiator (one line)

> Every adaptive app now claims it reshuffles your week. Streeka is the only one that does
> it *as a conversation* and *explains every decision* — it reads what you actually rode,
> then explains it in plain language, instead of handing you numbers to interpret alone.

## Nuance to protect

"Not dodgy metrics" is the right *enemy*, but Streeka **does** read real Garmin/Wahoo
data — that's a strength, not something to disown. (Streeka does **not** connect to Strava —
corrected 2026-06-19. Garmin + Wahoo OAuth only.) Message the distinction, never "no data":

- ✅ "Reads what you actually rode, then explains it in plain language."
- ✅ "You shouldn't need to interpret a wall of numbers."
- ❌ "Forget the data / no metrics." (reads as less capable, and it's untrue)

Real data **in**, a coach's reasoning **out**.

## Accuracy constraint — "chat", never "talk/voice" (2026-06-19)

SAMI is **text chat only** — there is **no voice input on iOS**. So the conversation pillar
must be worded as typing, not speaking:

- ✅ "an AI coach you **chat with**", "ask it in plain language" (typing implied)
- ❌ "an AI coach you **talk to**", "**talk to** SAMI", "say to SAMI" (implies voice — untrue)

Applies everywhere customer-facing: homepage, press page, outreach emails, funnel landers.

## Decisions applied (2026-06-19)

- **Hero lead:** conversation/explains (bolder, more defensible vs CCAI) — not the messy-week line.
- **Anti-metrics tone:** soft & implied — "decode a screen full of numbers," no naming TSS/CTL/ATL.

## Retired language (do not reuse as differentiator)

- "Adaptive training" — commoditised (2025).
- "Treat disruption as default" / "rebuilds your week when life gets in the way" — now being
  run verbatim by Cycling Coach AI. Still true, still usable as *support*, but no longer the wedge.
