# B. Instagram

Repurpose your existing reel backlog, don't film a new set for this. One main post a week is plenty,
captions below are seeds not scripts. **Launch post (B1) goes up as an Instagram Collab:** tag Pastries
as collaborator, they accept, and it lands on both profiles (roughly doubles reach).

### B1. Launch post, week of 28 June (carousel or reel)
> 16 weeks to Bowral. One distance to pick. A plan that survives a real week.
> The Pastries crew is riding to Bowral together this year (85, 120 or 150), each on a plan that bends
> around the group rides and the chaos of an actual life. I'm doing it too (still the first user of my
> own app, still getting humbled by Tuesdays).
> Members: 20% off, how to claim (iPhone or Android) at the link in bio. Pick your distance 👇
> #bowralclassic #cycling #roadtobowral

### B2. Weekly post angles (one per week, keep them light)
1. **Why we're doing this:** the club, the date, pick-your-distance.
2. **Choosing your distance:** honest take on 85 vs 120 vs 150.
3. **Turning a group ride into training:** the Pastries ride *is* the session.
4. **What happens when you miss a week:** the adapt story (your strongest angle, it's your ICP's #1 pain).
5. **Mid-block member moment:** a rider/ride leader, their distance, their goal (with consent).
6. **Pacing rolling terrain:** short, practical, Bowral-specific.
7. **Fuelling rehearsal:** what to practise before the day.
8. **4 weeks to go:** progress check, a number or two if you've got them.
9. **What tapering should feel like:** counterintuitive, shareable.
10. **Event-week checklist:** carousel, genuinely useful.
11. **Race-day:** photos/clips from Bowral.
12. **Recap:** what the club did, a result or two, a quiet nod to the app.

### B3. Race-day / recap caption seed
> The Pastries crew rode to Bowral. Months of plans that got rewritten every time life interfered, and
> everyone got to the line anyway. This is what training with your club actually looks like. 🥐🚴
