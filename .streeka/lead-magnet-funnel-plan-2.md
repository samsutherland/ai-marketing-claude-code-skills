## Guide Content Outline

**Target length:** 600–800 words. This is a cheat sheet, not a tutorial.

**1. What changes when the AI knows you**  
Generic: "What should a 45-year-old cyclist do to improve FTP?"  
Specific: "My FTP is 260w, I've been stagnating for 8 weeks, I missed 30% of sessions last month — what's actually going on?"  
The second question gets a real answer. The first gets advice for no one.  
Most cyclists never make this shift. They write to the AI like it's a search engine.

**2. The prompts that work (and what makes them work)**  
Show 4–5 real examples. Structure each as: the bad version → the good version → why it's different.  

Examples:
- Training block planning ("build me a 12-week plan" vs "I have 8 hours/week, my last block was interrupted 3 times, here's where my fitness sits — what's the right block structure?")
- Recovery decisions ("should I train today?" vs "I did 4h30 at threshold yesterday, slept 5h, HRV is down 12% — ride easy, skip, or full rest?")
- Race/event prep ("how do I prepare for a sportive?" vs "I have 11 weeks, my longest recent ride is 4h, the event is 6h with 3000m climbing — where are the gaps?")
- Diagnosing a plateau ("why am I not getting faster?" vs "I've done 3 months of zone 2, FTP hasn't moved, I've missed about 20% of sessions — what am I missing?")

**3. The problem with doing this manually**  
To get specific answers, you need to give specific context — every time.  
That means pasting numbers, recalling the last 6 weeks, remembering what you said last session.  
Most people don't. They write vague questions, get vague answers, give up.

**4. What Streeka does instead**  
It holds the context. Your data, your history, your fitness state — already there.  
The prompt engineering is already done. You just ask.  
Examples of what you'd type vs what a Streeka user types to get the same result.

**5. How to ask Streeka to do things**  
This section is explicit product education. Not a sales pitch — a reference list.  

Format: plain commands the reader can copy.  
- "What should I do this week given my last 3 sessions?"
- "I only have 45 minutes tomorrow — what's the most useful thing to do?"
- "I've missed 4 sessions this month. How do I recover the block?"
- "My legs feel heavy and I have a key ride on Saturday — should I take Wednesday easy?"
- "I want to build to [event]. Where do I start?"

**End of guide CTA:**  
> "Start your free trial — 2 weeks free, no card required."  
One link. Nothing else.
