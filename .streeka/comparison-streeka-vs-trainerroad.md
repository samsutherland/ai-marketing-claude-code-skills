## Page copy

# Streeka vs TrainerRoad

Both Streeka and TrainerRoad put AI at the centre of your training. They point it at different jobs.

TrainerRoad is a workout engine built to make you faster. Its AI runs simulations to pick the right structured workout for each ride, predicts how hard it'll feel, and adapts your plan as your fitness changes. If your goal is raw speed and you'll put in the interval work, it's one of the best in the world at it — 30 million workouts completed and a 4.9 rating say so.

Streeka points the AI at a different problem: being a coach you can actually talk to. It reads your rides, rebuilds your week when life gets in the way, and explains every change in plain language. Less "here's the optimal workout," more "here's what I'd do this week, and here's why."

## The short version

TrainerRoad makes the workout smarter. You follow a structured plan, its AI picks and adjusts the sessions to drive your FTP up, and you execute them indoors or out.

Streeka makes the coaching conversational. You don't just follow the plan — you talk to it. "I'm cooked, should I still do Saturday?" or "why is this week easier?" get real answers, grounded in what you rode. When your week falls apart, Streeka rebuilds it and tells you *why* each session changed, the way a coach would.

One optimises the workout. The other coaches the rider.

## Where TrainerRoad is stronger

Give it its due — TrainerRoad has the most mature training AI in cycling, full stop. It runs hundreds of simulations per ride, has an enormous structured-workout library, runs on basically everything (iOS, Android, Mac, Windows, Apple TV), works indoors and outside, and has a long, proven track record of putting watts on riders who do the work. If your single goal is to get faster through structured interval training, TrainerRoad is a serious tool and a fair pick.

Streeka isn't trying to out-engineer that. It's a different job: a coach you talk to, for the rider who wants guidance and context, not just the optimal next interval.

## Where Streeka is different

**1. You can talk to it.**
TrainerRoad's chat is customer support — it won't coach you. Streeka is a coach you chat with in plain language: "should I move the long ride, work blew up?" gets a straight, reasoned answer, grounded in what you actually rode. (Text chat — you type, no voice required.)

**2. It explains every decision.**
TrainerRoad adapts your plan, but it doesn't talk you through the reasoning — you get a new workout, not the *why*. When Streeka changes your week, it tells you what it saw, what it's protecting, and what it traded off. The goal isn't only a smart plan; it's finishing the week understanding it.

**3. It adapts around your life, not just your fitness.**
TrainerRoad's AI adapts the workouts to your *performance* — to make you faster. Streeka's adapts to your *week* — the missed session, the blown-out work day, the bad night's sleep — and reshuffles the plan around real life, then explains the call. Different kind of adaptation for a different kind of rider.

## Who should pick which

**TrainerRoad** is the right call if your number-one goal is getting faster through structured interval training, you'll commit to the workouts, and you want the most proven training AI and the deepest workout library. It's a powerhouse for the performance-focused rider.

**Streeka** is the better fit if you want a coach you can talk to — one that adapts your week around real life, answers your questions, and explains its reasoning in plain language. It's built for the desk-job cyclist who's serious about getting faster but has a genuinely messy schedule, and wants guidance and context, not just the optimal next interval.
